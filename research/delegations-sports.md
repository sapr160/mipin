## Conflicts and caveats

1. **Delegation count 37 vs 38 (Anguilla).** ES Wikipedia (XXV Juegos Centroamericanos y del Caribe) lists 38 delegations including "Anguila". The official jcc2026.org flag list (fetched 2026-07-23, during the Games), EN Wikipedia, Olympics.com, and Dominican press all say **37, without Anguilla**. Anguilla has no IOC-recognized NOC and does not appear on the official site; treat ES Wikipedia's 38 as erroneous/unconfirmed. Recommendation: ship 37.
2. **Discipline count 55 vs 56.** jcc2026.org and press say "40 deportes"; EN Wikipedia says 56 disciplines; the official site's own discipline dropdown contains **55** entries. The gap is likely a counting convention (e.g., wrestling's two styles, or BMX freestyle absent from the venue filter). Recommendation: ship the 55 official picker entries.
3. **Event count.** EN Wikipedia states 501 medal events; other aggregators (allsportdb) say 483. Not needed for picker data; unresolved.
4. **Name variants.** Official site uses "Guyana Francesa" (more standard ES: "Guayana Francesa"), "Beisbol" (no accent), "Islas Vírgenes de EE.UU.". ES Wikipedia uses "San Martín" for Sint Maarten — the CCS member is Dutch **Sint Maarten (SX)**, not French Saint-Martin (MF).
5. **ISO caveat.** All 37 delegations map cleanly to ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 codes (including territories: PR, CW, AW, BM, KY, VG, VI, GP, MQ, GF, SX, TC). None lack a code.
6. **Centro Caribe Sports member page** could not be retrieved (no /miembros page; homepage returns 403 to plain fetchers). Membership was corroborated via the official Games site instead.
7. **In-Games status notes (2026-07-22, jcc2026.org):** Guyana's flag at half-mast; Venezuela competing with black crespón on uniforms. No delegation withdrawals reported as of 2026-07-23.
